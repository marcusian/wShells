# NGC 3656


## Interest

- Shell velocities
    - Bright southern shell (tentative detection with WHT/INTEGRAL, 2001)
    - Kinematic signatures in main-body shells
- Nuclear starbust
- Regions with [NII] > H$\alpha$. Shock-excited gas 

## WEAVE data

- LR four pointings, with much overlap, 2-hr integration on each.
- HR (G+R) one pointing centered on nucleus

### Southern shell - wedge kinematic profiles (posang -90 deg)

Stellar velocity at the southern shell clearly measured: Vshell = 68 km/s above systemic (80 km/s in some spaxels). 

Stellar velocity dispersion at the southern shell is around 50 km/s. At the limit of the detection with the LR mode of the LIFU. 

Velocity profile rises steadily from 20 arcsec outward: southern shell does not have distinct velocity from the galaxy main body.

### Shell in main body, S-W of nucleus - wedge kinematic profiles (posang -40 deg)

*Velocity profile follows the shell residual surface brightness:* minima at radii 15-20 arcsec, maxima at radius 30 arcsec, the shell boundary. 


| Wedge Profiles | Wedge Profiles |
| --- | --- |
| ![[ngc3656_lr_ob13787_pam40_aper40_rmax40_wed.png]] | ![[ngc3656sh_lr_ob14586_pam40_aper40_rmax40_wed.png]] |
| ![[ngc3656ssh_lr_ob16851_pam40_aper40_rmax40_wed.png]] | ![[ngc3656nw_lr_ob16852_pam40_aper40_rmax40_wed.png]] |



The pattern is seen in the four spectra, which gives some reassurance it is real. In fact in the velocity maps of [the stellar kinematics maps](#stellar-kinematics-maps) show arc-shaped patterns of distinct velocity at the same location SW of the galaxy center. 
See the example below too:

![[ngc3656sh_lr_ob14586_VelStar.png]]

The profiles of dispersion, H3 and H4 are plotted to search for any patterns coincident with the shells. None is seen in a consistent way across the four data sets. Other than perhaps a sharp H4 peak at radius 10 arcsec. SNR is probably too low to get realiable H3, H4.
