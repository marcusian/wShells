# Science Justification

## Goals

1. IFU mapping of cores of galaxies with narrow velocity features. targets: NGC 474, NGC 7626. 
2. Re-measure line-of-sight velocities of outer shells published to date. Targets: NGC 474; NGC 3656. 
3. Get stellar population diagnostics in and around shells. Targets: NGC 474, NGC 3656, NGC 5982, NGC 7626
4. Get gas excitation diagnostics in galaxies with optical emission lines. Targets: NGC 3656.

## Motivation

- **Shell galaxies** provide good case studies of the merger process: merger dynamics and impact on galaxy evolution. Work in the 1980's and 1990's was based on interpreting imaging data: shell morphologies, plus a bit of shell colours, but not on kinematics or populations.

- Examples of our work on shell galaxies in the 1990's and 2000's, led by the late David Carter:
    - Spectroscopy of nuclear regions with WHT/ISIS. This revealed cases of **peculiar core kinematics** (Balcells & Carter, NGC 7626, Oosterloo et al. on NGC 5982, Hau et al on NGC 474
    - Stellar population studies of the cores (Hau et al, NGC474; NGC 2685).
    - Morphology and colours of nuclear shells with HST imaging (Sikkema et al 2007, NGC 474, 1344, 2865, 3923, 5982, 7626).

- **Kinematic information on shell galaxies** is valuable to understand the merger dynamics, and has received little attention. Probably, because shells are typically expected to leave no measurable kinematic signature owing to their low relative surface brightness of a few \% of that of the underlying galaxy. Only with the advent of IFU spectrographs on 10m-class telescopes have studies of shell kinematics started (VLT/MUSE: Fensch et al 2020 on NGC 474). There has been earlier work though, which we believe can be expanded nowadays. 
    - The WHT/ISIS high-spatial resolution spectroscopy of elliptical nuclei revealed **spatially-narrow velocity features (NVF)** in the nuclei of NGC 7626 (Balcells & Carter 1993) and the nucleus of NGC 474 (Hau et al 1966). These were tentatively named 'kinematic shells' and were hypothesized to provide nuclear traces of the merger process, in a similar way as morphological shells gave signatures of mergers in the outer parts. 
    - With **WHT/WYFFOS/INTEGRAL**, a measurement of the velocity of the outer, south shell of NGC 3656 was published (Balcells et al 2001). HI was also detected in this galaxy (VLA) making it an interesting case for **shell formation in a gas-rich merger**. If line-of-sight shell velocities are equal to systemic, then the shell was formed by **phase-wrapping**; otherwise, the shell material has tangential motions hence the shell was formed by **spatial wrapping**. But the optical velocity error bars were large - more data was necessary. 
    - The WEAVE large integral-field unit (LIFU), with its 90-arcsec FOV, wide wavelength range (3700 - 9500 $\mathring{A}$ ) and exquisite spectral resolution (R = 2500 or 10,000) should be able to provide new information on 

        - The kinematics of shells
        - Stellar populations
        - ISM excitation and extinction
        - Extinction
